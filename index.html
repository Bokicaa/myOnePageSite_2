WHO
WE
ARE

Founded in 2009 in Jackson Hole, WY, Purple Orange LLC produces small batch, high quality brand communications mixed with creativity, modern mediums and a respected authentic voice. Our professional influences range from publishing, large and small agency work, and former in-house marketing; we like to think we see the whole picture for our clients. Specializing in active lifestyle and natural living, our clients have spanned industry-leading performance apparel brands, food and beverage, consumer tech, and non-profit work.

02

MEANINGFUL COMMUNICATIONS

Believe Good PR Is About Meaningful Communication.

It’s no longer enough to make a good product. People — especially millennials — want to feel positive connections to the brands and products they use.

We look for ways to distill the complex, create stories that connect, and share them with journalists who trust us.

Peak Design gave 100% of their sales to environmental non-profits on Earth Day. Sales that day bounced up 85% over average and generated intense interest in the media.

OLD FASHION FACE-TO-FACE COMMUNICATION

Despite all the modes of digital communication these days, old fashion face-to-face is often still the best. Depending on your goals, we can set up a big bash VIP media party, schedule individual deskside appointments with your dream editors, or brainstorm something that helps your brand stand out at an important tradeshow. It all depends what’s best for you.

BRANDED MEDIA TRIPS: SHOW DON'T TELL

“We want to stop telling people how it works and show them.” — Andy Cohrane, Oru Kayak

This is what Oru Kayak told us when they approached Purple Orange to handle their PR. The company that gave birth to a completely new breed of kayak wanted to get journalists out on the water — regularly. So together we compiled an ambitious event program (nearly once a month), traveling to bucket-list locations around the world with Oru kayaks and top-tier journalists in tow.

The result? Amazing PR that truly captures the adventure and joy of traveling in an Oru Kayak.

NYC MARATHON TRAINING

In an effort to create a more meaningful experience to wear performance compression socks, Purple Orange partnered with Rock ‘n Roll Marathon Series to help a group of New York based journalists train and finish a half marathon race. As part of the 3-month training program, Purple Orange arranged to have a local NY-based trainer work one-on-one with the journalists to help prepare them for the race. We also provided complimentarily running shoes, apparel, sports nutrition, and wearable tech watches to follow their progress.

WINTER BREAK

Purple Orange’s annual Winter Break media event provides the perfect platform to experience some of the most innovative brands in modern snowsports. From sports nutrition, to winter apparel, skis, boots, and the latest in outdoor tech, top journalists from around the country have the opportunity to ski with product designers, play with next year’s gear, and experience the unique western heritage that Jackson Hole has to offer.

CELEBRATING 250,000 LBS OF TRASH

United By Blue picks up a lot of trash; one pound for every product they sell. We brought in a handful of NYC editors to join us for an evening event at the company’s hip old town Philadelphia HQ, and to join the company as they hosted a local waterway cleanup effort expected to clear the 250K threshold.

So did United By Blue reach the quarter million benchmark? Read about it for yourself in The Guardian.

Peak Design launched on Kickstarter in 2010, just one year after the crowdfunding platform was founded. At the time, the brand raised nearly $360K in funding — not a huge amount by modern standards, but in 2010 it tallied as the second most funded campaign to-date. Since then, Peak Design has come back to the platform once a year, for 6 years straight, each time bringing real innovation and fueling the company’s fan-base.

Today, Peak Design has transformed itself from a one-trick pony to one of the most envied brands in all of crowdfunding. Peak Design finished their 2016 campaign with over $7.2M in crowdfunding and more than 29,000 customers capitalizing on the pre-sales. Total all Peak Design’s campaigns, and you’ll find they are they most crowdfunded brand in business.

Purple Orange has been the AOR for Peak Design on 4 out of 6 campaigns over the last 6 years. With limited product samples and racing against the clock, each year Purple Orange has delivered the kind of national media attention that drives revenue into the eight figures.

Oru Kayak came to us in the beginning of 2017 asking for a robust experiential public relation program based on showing people how the company’s innovative boats work, not just telling them. Working together with Oru Kayak, Purple Orange helped organize and execute 7 media events over the course of the year — ranging from kayaking and deep-sea climbing in Indonesia to road tripping across the Olympic Peninsula in Airstream trailers.

The result was a superior brand of PR based on life-changing on-the-water experiences rather than email correspondence. Oru Kayaks have the ability to go where no other boats can, and they perform in a playful unexpected way. Combined with unparalleled ease-of-carry and beautiful design, we successfully wooed editors from across the spectrum as to why they should dedicate full-page editorial spreads to these funky foldable boats.

CHRIS DICKEY

Founder + Principal

Purple Orange LLC

Chris applies a liberal arts perspective to Purple Orange as a result of a diverse marketing background spanning manufacturing, publishing, and agency work. Early on, Chris served as the first Marketing and Sales Director for then-startup NEMO Equipment, then onto Alpinist Magazine in the role of Marketing and Circulation Director before finally transitioning into agency work with both Stanwood and Partners and Carmichael Lynch.

Chris has helped some of the largest names in the outdoor and active lifestyle industry successfully execute North American brand communication campaigns including Arc’teryx Equipment, Atomic Skis, Jack Links Beef Jerky, and Rollerblade USA.

Chris currently lives in Jackson Hole, WY where he can mountain bike at lunch and climb and ski in the Tetons year round. He is a published writer, award- winning filmmaker, and blogger.
